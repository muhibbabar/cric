Fun facts about Cricket palyers🏏!

Rohit sharma ▼
Sharma is the only player to have scored three double hundreds in ODI cricket; no other player has scored even two. He also holds the record for most international sixes across all formats, most sixes in World Cups, and the highest individual score in an ODI!
Jasprit Bumrah ▼
Bumrah was born into a Sikh family in Ahmedabad, Gujarat. He lost his father at the age of five, and the family faced financial struggles in his early years. Bumrah has had a reputation for bowling fast since his childhood days.
Virat Kohli ▼
Kohli was highest run scorer of 2023 ICC World Cup and was named Player of the Tournament. Kohli is only player to have won two Player of the Tournament in the history of the T20 World Cup, in 2014 and 2016 editions. At 2023 World Cup Semi Final against New Zealand
Mohammed Rizwan ▼
He has scored centuries in all three formats: Tests, One Day Internationals and Twenty20 Internationals. He holds the record for most runs in T20 Internationals in a calendar year with 1326 runs in 26 innings at an average of 73.66.
Shahid Afridi ▼
In his first international innings, Afridi broke the record for fastest century in ODI cricket, reaching his hundred from 37 balls. The eleven sixes he struck also equaled the record for most in an ODI innings.
Shaheen Shah Afridi ▼
Shaheen Shah Afridi is a Pakistani cricketer who has made significant contributions to the sport. He is the youngest cricketer to take a five-wicket haul in a World Cup match, and holds the record for best bowling figures by a Pakistani bowler on his first-class debut match.
Babar Azam ▼
He is the third-fastest Pakistani batsman to reach 1000 runs in ODI cricket (21 innings). He is also the joint fastest Pakistani batsman to reach 2000 runs in ODI cricket (45 innings), along with Zaheer Abbas. Babar Azam is also the second fastest Asian batsman to reach 3000 runs in ODI cricket .
Naseem Shah ▼
He became the youngest fast bowler in Test history to take a five-wicket haul, doing so in Karachi against Sri Lanka in just his second match. Records continued to tumble, with Naseem becoming the youngest to take a Test match hat-trick in his third Test against Bangladesh.
Haris Rauf
▼
He remains sought after in T20 leagues around the world, and is also a fixture in the Pakistan side, and became just the second bowler from the country to take 100 wickets in the shortest format after Shadab Khan.
Mohammed Amir
▼
In the summer of 2010, Amir was awarded the man of the match award for becoming the youngest player ever to take a five-wicket haul in England. He also became the youngest player to take 50 test wickets, earning praise from several former international cricketers.

Lets see how Cricket works!

A batsman may hit right-handed or left-handed. Good batting is based on a straight (i.e., vertical) bat with its full face presented to the ball, although a cross (i.e., horizontal) bat can be used effectively to deal with short bowling.
Bowling, in cricket, is the action of propelling the ball toward the wicket defended by a batter. A player skilled at bowling is called a bowler;[1] a bowler who is also a competent batter is known as an all-rounder. Bowling the ball is distinguished from throwing the ball by a strictly specified biomechanical definition, which restricts the angle of extension of the elbow.[2] A single act of bowling the ball towards the batter is called a ball or a delivery. Bowlers bowl deliveries in sets of six, called an over.
Fielding refers to the act of catching, collecting and returning a ball that has been hit by the batsman. There are nine fielders on each team, as one player out of the 11 will have to act as the bowler, while another takes up the role of the wicket keeper.
